## What changed (in `AppShell.tsx`, plus the logo asset)

**1. Real customer logo in the header.** Copied your logo into the app (`src/assets/madison-logo.webp`, bundled as a hashed asset so it travels with the repo) and replaced the placeholder "M" wordmark with it, followed by a thin divider + "Command Center" so the product name is retained.

**2. Made it work with the colors/background — your "is it right to use" question.** Your logo has a red/crimson "MM" mark with **black** "MADISON" text on a transparent background. On a dark canvas that black text would disappear. So I placed the logo on a clean **white lockup tile** (rounded, subtle ring/shadow) — a standard logo treatment that keeps both the red mark and the black wordmark fully legible on **both** dark and light themes.

**3. Defaults set to Dark + Color-Vision Friendly,** as requested — the app now opens in dark theme with the color-vision-friendly palette active, both still toggleable from the **Display** menu.

One deliberate call worth flagging: I did **not** repaint the whole UI red to match the logo. The logo's red is very close to the universal "error/alert" red, so using it for primary buttons and active nav would muddy status signals (an alert and a nav item would read the same). I kept the clinical teal as the functional accent and let the logo carry the brand identity on its own tile — which is why it reads cleanly against the current background. If you'd prefer the chrome to lean into the crimson, that's a quick follow-up — just say the word and I'll retune the brand tokens (keeping error-red distinct).
